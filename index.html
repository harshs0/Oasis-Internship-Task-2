Cristiano Ronaldo

The Greatest of All Time

Early Life and Career

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was born on February 5, 1985, in Madeira, Portugal. He grew up in a working-class family and began playing soccer at a young age.

At the age of 16, Ronaldo was signed to Sporting CP's youth academy. He made his first team debut in 2002 and quickly established himself as a rising star.

Club Career

In 2003, Ronaldo signed with Manchester United, where he spent six seasons and won three Premier League titles, one UEFA Champions League title, and one FIFA Club World Cup.

In 2009, he transferred to Real Madrid for a world record transfer fee of €94 million. Ronaldo spent nine seasons with Real Madrid, winning four UEFA Champions League titles and two La Liga titles.

In 2018, Ronaldo signed with Juventus for a fee of €100 million. He spent three seasons with the club before returning to Manchester United in 2021.

International Career

Ronaldo has also had a successful international career with the Portugal national team. He made his debut in 2003 and has since become the team's all-time leading scorer and most-capped player.

In 2016, Ronaldo helped lead Portugal to their first major international trophy, winning the UEFA European Championship.
